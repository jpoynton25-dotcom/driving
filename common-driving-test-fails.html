Practical test guide

Common Driving Test Fails and How to Fix Them

If you have failed a practical test, this page shows the high-frequency faults that keep appearing and what to train before rebooking.

See top fail reasons Get practical test support plan

Most fails are preventable

  • Find the pattern behind each serious or repeated fault
  • Train with clear drills, not random driving
  • Retake only when your weak areas are controlled
  • Use your test report as a revision checklist
Top fail reasons

Frequent practical test faults

👀

Observation at junctions

Rushing out, poor checks, or moving without a clear safe gap.

🪞

Mirrors before changing speed or direction

Missing mirror checks before signaling, braking, or lane changes.

🧭

Positioning and lane discipline

Late lane choice, drifting, or weak roundabout positioning decisions.

⚙️

Control under pressure

Stalling, harsh clutch use, or rushed gear changes in busy traffic.

Fix plan

Use this retake structure

  • Review your previous test report and rank faults by safety risk
  • Run focused sessions for each weak area with repetition
  • Simulate real test pressure with independent driving practice
  • Do one full mock route before you rebook

Retake timing rule

Rebook when your recurring faults stop repeating across multiple sessions, not just one good drive.
